How to Report a Food Safety Issue

Document the Product

Take clear photos of the product, including the label, batch number, expiration date, and any visible issues with the product. Keep the original packaging.

Preserve Evidence

If possible, store the product in a sealed container in the refrigerator or freezer to preserve it for testing if needed.

Contact the Manufacturer

Reach out to the manufacturer using the contact information on the package. Document this communication.

Report to Regulatory Agencies

Use the appropriate contact information above to report to the FDA, USDA, or other relevant authority.

Seek Medical Attention if Needed

If you've consumed the product and experienced health issues, see a healthcare provider immediately.

Follow Up

Keep records of all communications and follow up with the agencies if you don't receive a response.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you suspect a counterfeit food product:

  1. Do not consume the product
  2. Document all details including where and when you purchased it
  3. Report to the FDA using the Food Safety Hotline at 1-888-SAFEFOOD
  4. Contact the legitimate manufacturer to report the counterfeit
  5. Report to your local health department
  6. If you've already consumed the product and feel ill, seek medical attention immediately

To file a complaint about false advertising on food labels:

  1. Gather evidence of the false claim (photos, packaging, receipts)
  2. Report to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at 1-877-FTC-HELP or online at reportfraud.ftc.gov
  3. File a complaint with your state's Attorney General's office
  4. Consider contacting a consumer protection attorney if you've suffered damages
  5. For class action potential, websites like classaction.org can help connect you with relevant legal resources

Common food labeling violations include:

  • Undeclared allergens - Not listing major allergens like nuts, dairy, or gluten
  • Nutritional misrepresentation - Inaccurate calorie counts or nutrient amounts
  • False health claims - Unsubstantiated "reduces heart disease" or similar claims
  • Misleading ingredient lists - Not listing ingredients in order of predominance
  • "Natural" claims - Using "natural" when products contain synthetic ingredients
  • Country of origin fraud - Misrepresenting where food was grown or produced
  • Organic mislabeling - Using the term "organic" without certification

To check for food recalls:

  • Visit FoodSafety.gov for a comprehensive list of recalls
  • Check the FDA's recall list at FDA Recalls
  • For meat, poultry, and egg products, visit the USDA FSIS Recalls
  • Sign up for email alerts from FoodSafety.gov
  • Download the FoodKeeper App which includes recall notifications

When reporting a food safety issue, have the following information ready:

  • Product name and brand
  • Package size and type
  • Manufacturing codes (usually found on the package)
  • Expiration or "best by" date
  • UPC code (barcode number)
  • Where and when you purchased the product
  • Nature of the complaint or issue you observed
  • Whether you or others consumed the product
  • Any symptoms experienced
  • Photos of the product and labeling if available
